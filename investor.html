2. INTRODUCTION

2.1 The purpose of this Agreement is to regulate the subscription for Ordinary Shares by the Subscriber and the issue and allotment of such shares by the Company.

2.2 The Company has agreed to issue the Subscription Shares, and the Subscriber has agreed to subscribe at the Subscription Price.

2.3 The Parties wish to enter into this Agreement to record and implement the terms and conditions relating to the subscription of the Ordinary Shares.

3. CONDITIONS PRECEDENT

3.1 This Agreement is subject to the fulfilment or waiver of the following Conditions Precedent by not later than the Closing Date:

3.1.1 The MOI shall have been amended in accordance with the Companies Act to accommodate the creation of the Ordinary Shares;

3.1.2 All required board and shareholder resolutions have been validly adopted;

3.1.3 The board of directors has approved the issue of the Subscription Shares;

3.1.4 The board has conducted the solvency and liquidity test in accordance with the Companies Act;

3.1.5 The Subscriber concludes a deed of adherence to be party to the Shareholders Agreement;

3.1.6 The shareholders provide a written waiver waiving any rights of first refusal or pre-emption.

4. CREATION OF THE ORDINARY SHARES

The Company shall procure that its MOI is amended by filing with the Companies and Intellectual Property Registration Office within 21 days after the Signature Date for the creation of the Ordinary Shares.

5. SUBSCRIPTION OF SHARES

5.1 The Subscriber hereby subscribes for the Subscription Shares with effect from the Subscription Date.

5.2 The Subscriber shall pay the Subscription Price in immediately available funds into the Designated Account on the Subscription Date.

5.3 All risk and benefit attaching to the Subscription Shares shall pass to the Subscriber with effect from the Effective Date.

6. SUBSCRIPTION PRICE AND PAYMENT TERMS

6.1 The Subscription Price for each Ordinary Share shall be R62.61 (sixty-two Rand and sixty-one cents).

6.2 The Subscription Price constitutes adequate consideration in terms of section 40 of the Companies Act, and all Ordinary Shares will be issued as fully paid shares for cash.

6.3 All payments shall be made by electronic transfer of immediately available funds, free of any deductions or set-off, in South African currency, into the Designated Account.

6.4 Upon receipt of the Subscription Amount in cleared funds, ownership of the Subscription Shares shall pass from the Company to the Subscriber with effect from the Effective Date.

7. ALLOTMENT AND ISSUE OF THE SUBSCRIPTION SHARES

7.1 Upon subscription and payment, the Company shall:

7.1.1 On the Subscription Date, allot and issue the Subscription Shares to the Subscriber;

7.1.2 Procure that the Subscriber is reflected in the Company's securities register as the registered holder of the Subscription Shares;

7.1.3 Deliver to the Subscriber original share certificates, board resolutions, and other necessary documents.

8. INDEMNITIES

8.1 The Company and Shareholders indemnify the Subscriber against all actual or contingent losses, liabilities, damages, and costs arising from:

8.1.1 The creation, allotment and issue of the Subscription Shares being set aside or deemed unlawful;

8.1.2 Any liability of the Company arising prior to the Subscription Date;

8.1.3 Any breach of contract or other cause arising prior to the Effective Date.

9. WARRANTIES

9.1 Each Party warrants that it has the legal capacity to enter into and perform this Agreement.

9.2 The Company warrants that the Subscription Shares will be validly issued and rank pari passu with all other shares, and no person shall have any right to acquire them.

10. SUPPORT

The Parties undertake to do all things necessary for the putting into effect and maintenance of the terms and conditions of this Agreement.

11. BREACH

If any Party breaches a material provision and fails to remedy it within 10 Business Days of written notice, the Non-Defaulting Party may cancel this Agreement or claim specific performance.

12. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

12.1 Disputes shall be resolved by negotiation.

12.2 One Party shall invite the other in writing to meet within 14 days.

12.3 If unresolved after 14 days, disputes shall be submitted to arbitration before a Senior Advocate with 15 years' experience.

12.4 The Uniform Rules of Court apply. Arbitration shall be conducted in English in Johannesburg.

12.5 The Arbitrator's decision is final and binding.

13. NOTICES AND DOMICILIA

Each Party chooses as their domicilium citandi et executandi the addresses set forth in clause 1. Notices must be in writing and may be by email. Any notice actually received is adequate notice.

14. INDEPENDENT ADVICE

Each Party acknowledges it has been free to secure independent legal, financial, and tax advice, and that all provisions are fair and reasonable.

15. GENERAL

15.1 Whole Agreement: This Agreement constitutes the sole agreement and supersedes all prior representations.

15.2 Provisions Severable: Invalid provisions are severed without invalidating the remainder.

15.3 No Indulgences: No relaxation of terms constitutes a waiver of rights.

15.4 Variations in Writing: No variation is effective unless in writing and signed by authorized representatives.

16. GOVERNING LAW

This Agreement is governed by the laws of the Republic of South Africa, including its prescription laws but excluding its conflict of law principles.

17. COSTS

Each Party bears its own costs in negotiation, preparation, and signature of this Agreement.

18. SIGNATURE

18.1 This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each deemed an original.

18.2 It is not required that parties initial each page or have signatures verified by a witness.
